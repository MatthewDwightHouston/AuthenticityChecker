Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas is a novel by Hunter S. Thompson that follows the misadventures of Raoul Duke and his attorney, Dr. Gonzo, as they travel to Las Vegas. Through the use of drugs and alcohol, they engage in a wild, surrealistic journey and explore the darker side of the city. The novel is an exploration of the concept of the "American Dream," as well as a satirical look at the excesses of the counterculture of the 1960s.
